May Brief — Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 15, 2026

May Brief is a personal news briefing app. Your privacy is important — here's exactly what the app does and doesn't do.

What We Collect

Location (when-in-use only)
Used solely to display local weather and area name. Your location is never stored, transmitted to third parties, or linked to your identity.

How the App Works

  • The app fetches a pre-generated HTML briefing from a private GitHub repository.
  • Weather data is fetched using your approximate location via wttr.in (no personal data sent).
  • Briefing content is cached locally on your device for offline reading.
  • Text-to-speech uses the device's built-in speech engine (no audio sent externally).

What We Don't Do

  • No analytics or tracking SDKs
  • No advertising
  • No user accounts or authentication
  • No data sharing with third parties
  • No cookies or fingerprinting
  • No personal data collection beyond location for weather

Data Storage

All data stays on your device. Cached briefings are stored in the app's sandboxed Documents directory and can be cleared by deleting the app.

Third-Party Services

  • GitHub API — to fetch briefing content (no personal data sent)
  • wttr.in — to fetch weather data (receives only coordinates, no identifying info)

Children's Privacy

This app is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from children.

Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted here with a new effective date.
